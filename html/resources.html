Resources

Want to learn how to code, but don't know where to start? You've come to the right place.

freeCodeCamp

freeCodeCamp is a non-profit organization that consists of an interactive learning web platform, an online community forum, chat rooms, online publications and local organizations that intend to make learning web development accessible to anyone. freeCodeCamp specializes in web development, so their (self-paced) curricula entails HTML, CSS, JavaScript, a multitude of APIs and front end libraries, and even Python. As the name implies, it is completely free, so it's worth a shot. Speaking from personal experience, it is a great way to learn.

W3Schools

W3Schools is a free training website for learning web technologies online. Content includes tutorials and references relating to HTML, CSS, JavaScript, JSON, PHP, Python, AngularJS, React.js, SQL, Bootstrap, Sass, Node.js, jQuery, XQuery, AJAX, XML, Raspberry Pi, C++, C# and Java. As you can tell, W3Schools offers a huge variety of content relating to whichever language or subject under computer science that you would like to learn. Due to this, we highly recommend them as a resource! The content on W3Schools is very easily accessible and is a great place to look if you are interested in a specific topic or language.

CodeHS

CodeHS is an interactive online learning platform offering computer science and programming instruction for schools and individual learners. CodeHS is focused on spreading access to and knowledge of computer science by offering online instructional materials supported by remote tutors. CodeHS offers a variety of programming languages, such as Java, JavaScript, Python, HTML, CSS, SQL, C++, C, and even languages like Karel for beginners. At Myers Park, CodeHS is utilized in our AP Computer Science and AP Computer Science A courses to supplement learning. CodeHS is great if you are a beginner, offerring an intro course using Karel for free, however other courses are locked behind a paywall.

Codecademy

Codecademy is an American online interactive platform that offers free coding classes in 12 different programming languages including Python, Java, Go, JavaScript, Ruby, SQL, C++, C#, Swift, and Sass, as well as markup languages HTML and CSS. Codecademy is very popular and provides a unique array of languages, making it a good option. Although Codecademy offers free content, they offer exclusive content to those who pay, so bear that in mind.

Massive Open Online Courses (MOOC)

Massive Online Open Courses (MOOC) are courses which aim to provided unlimited participation from users across the Internet. They are similar to these other resources, however they are typically offered by well-respected companies and universities such as Google, IBM, Stanford, MIT, Harvard, among many others. There are many different services which offer MOOCs, with the well known ones being edX, Coursera, Udemy, Udacity, and DataCamp. You can learn nearly any language or concept in computer science using these courses, and are often rewarded with a certificate for completing them. The companies and institutions offering these courses are usually very credible and therefore are great ways to learn.

YouTube

Obviously, YouTube is an online video sharing platform designed to allow users to watch and share all types of videos, including educational videos about computer science and programming. Due to the nature of YouTube, you can learn virtually any language you want. All the content on YouTube is free, which is a plus, however the content is not as interactive because they are just videos. That being said, there is a massive amount of content on YouTube, so it's still a solid resource.

You can also try taking a class (Myers Park has some) or doing a coding bootcamp. If you ever have any coding questions, feel free to reach out to us!
